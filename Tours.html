-Rome, the Eternal City, is a treasure trove of ancient history and modern culture. Explore iconic landmarks such as the Colosseum, Roman Forum, and Palatine Hill, which offer a glimpse into the glory of the Roman Empire.

-Florence, the cradle of the Renaissance, is renowned for its artistic and architectural masterpieces.
Visit the Uffizi Gallery to see works by Botticelli, Leonardo da Vinci, and Michelangelo, then admire the stunning beauty of the Florence Cathedral and Giotto's Bell Tower.

-Venice, the city of canals and romance, is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its unique architecture and timeless beauty. Explore St. Mark's Square and the Doge's Palace, then embark on a gondola ride along the picturesque canals, passing under elegant bridges and past centuries-old palaces.

-Siena is a medieval gem known for its stunning cathedral, Piazza del Campo, and Palio horse race. Explore the narrow streets of the historic center and climb the Torre del Mangia for panoramic views of the city.

-The Cinque Terre is a UNESCO World Heritage site consisting of five colorful villages perched on the rugged cliffs of the Ligurian coast.

-Return to Rome for your final day in Italy, where you can explore the vibrant neighborhoods of
Trastevere and Monti, shop for souvenirs at local markets, and enjoy a farewell dinner at a traditional Roman restaurant.
